Laguna: A City of Wonders

Immerse yourself in the beauty and culture of Laguna, Laguna

History

A Glimpse into the Past

Laguna, known as the "Enterprise City of the South," has a rich history dating back to its establishment in the early Spanish period. It was named after the kabuyaw tree, which once flourished in the area.

Landmarks and Attractions

What to See and Explore

The St. Polycarp Parish Church stands as a testament to Laguna's spiritual heritage. Other notable landmarks include the Enchanted Kingdom amusement park and the bustling Laguna market.

The Laguna People's Park offers a serene escape with lush greenery and recreational activities for families.

Local Delicacies

A Taste of Laguna

Laguna is home to delicious delicacies such as buko pie, cassava cake, and other traditional Filipino sweets. Local eateries also offer an array of savory dishes like sinigang and adobo.

Festivals and Events

Celebrating Culture and Community

The annual "Banamos Festival" showcases Laguna's pride in its rich culture and traditions. With parades, concerts, and food fairs, it brings the community together in a vibrant celebration.
